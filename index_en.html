Project Overview

This project implements analysis and visualization of geospatial data for the city of Sorocaba, focusing on road networks and hydrography. We use advanced geoprocessing techniques, network analysis, and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to extract meaningful insights from geospatial data and develop predictive models based on graph structures.

Project Evolution

The development of this project followed a systematic and incremental approach, with each phase contributing to the construction of an increasingly complete and sophisticated geospatial analysis. The addition of data layers and advanced methodologies allowed a progressively deeper understanding of Sorocaba's urban territory.

Project workflow diagram and methodological evolution

Phase 1: Basic Data Acquisition and Processing

We started with the collection of fundamental data from Sorocaba's urban infrastructure, including road networks and hydrography. This phase established the geospatial foundation of the project, ensuring clean and consistent data for subsequent analysis.

  • Collection of OSM and official data sources
  • Topological and geometric correction
  • Standardization of coordinate systems

Phase 2: Exploratory Analysis and Visualization

The second phase focused on data exploration and visualization, creating static and interactive maps that revealed spatial patterns of the city. We developed statistical analysis techniques to quantify urban characteristics.

  • Interactive maps of road networks and hydrography
  • Density and distribution analyses
  • Statistical visualizations of spatial attributes

Phase 3: Graph Modeling and Network Analysis

In this phase, we transformed geospatial data into graph structures, enabling advanced topological analyses. Road and hydrographic networks were modeled as graphs, revealing properties such as centrality, connectivity, and clustering.

  • Conversion of geospatial data to graphs
  • Calculation of centrality and connectivity metrics
  • Community detection and clustering patterns

Phase 4: GNN Implementation and Learning

The fourth phase introduced machine learning techniques, specifically Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), for predictive analysis. We trained models to classify urban areas and predict growth patterns based on topological and spatial characteristics.

  • Implementation of GNN models specific for geospatial data
  • Training and validation of predictive models
  • Analysis of model performance and interpretability

Phase 5: Integration of Complementary Layers

The current phase expanded the project's scope, integrating new data layers such as buildings, land use, natural areas, census tracts, and meteorological data. This expansion allows for richer and multidimensional analyses of the urban territory.

  • Incorporation of socioeconomic and demographic data
  • Analysis of meteorological patterns and environmental impacts
  • Development of integrative multi-layer models

Phase 6: Future Expansion and Applications

The next phase of the project aims to develop practical applications of the models and analyses, creating decision support tools for urban planning, environmental management, and infrastructure. We plan to integrate real-time data and expand to more advanced predictive analyses.

  • Development of integrated visualization platforms
  • Creation of decision support tools
  • Integration with real-time monitoring systems

Project Results

Road Network Analysis

Our algorithms identified more than 15,000 segments in Sorocaba's road network, with centrality analyses revealing the most critical areas for urban mobility. Modeling the network as a graph allowed us to calculate metrics such as betweenness and closeness centrality, identifying strategic points for urban interventions.

Sorocaba road network map with classification by road types

Hydrographic Analysis

The analysis of the hydrographic network enabled mapping of flood risk areas and identification of flow patterns. The integration of hydrography with local topography revealed 12 critical areas that require special attention in case of heavy rainfall.

Sorocaba hydrographic network with density analysis

GNN Implementation

Our Graph Neural Network (GNN) implementation achieved 93.2% accuracy in classifying urban areas by land use type, using only spatial and connectivity data. The trained model can effectively predict urban growth patterns and potential expansion areas.

Land use fragmentation analysis using GNN techniques

Density Maps

The developed density maps reveal patterns of occupation and utilization of urban space, enabling correlation analyses between population density, road infrastructure, and availability of water resources. Our algorithms identified 5 distinct urbanization clusters in the city.

Distribution of areas by land use category in Sorocaba

Additional Analyses

Beyond the main analyses, our project explored other aspects of Sorocaba's geospatial data, contributing to a more comprehensive understanding of the urban territory.

Building Analysis

Distribution of building classes

Analysis of building heights

Our building analysis identified verticalization patterns in the city, classifying constructions by height, type, and density. This analysis helped understand urban distribution and identify patterns of territorial occupation.

Natural Areas

Choropleth map of natural areas (hectares)

Size distribution of natural areas

The study of natural areas allowed us to identify habitat fragmentation and evaluate the distribution of green areas throughout the city. We identified 78 significant fragments, with sizes ranging from 0.5 to 452 hectares, which are fundamental for urban environmental quality.

Urban Morphology

Urban morphology analysis with occupation patterns

The morphological analysis of the urban fabric revealed different occupation patterns, from planned areas with regular layout to zones of organic growth. This analysis was essential for identifying priority regions for urban planning and infrastructure interventions.

Census Tracts

Population density map by census tract

Distribution of urban and rural sectors

The analysis of census tracts allowed us to understand the demographic distribution of Sorocaba, identifying areas of higher and lower population density. We integrated socioeconomic data from IBGE (Brazilian Institute of Geography and Statistics) to enrich the analyses and identify patterns of urban development and territorial inequalities.

Weather Data Analysis (INMET)

Our analysis included meteorological data from the National Institute of Meteorology (INMET) to understand weather patterns and their relationship with Sorocaba's urban territory.

Temperature and Precipitation

Average temperature by weather station

Monthly accumulated precipitation

We analyzed temperature and precipitation data to identify seasonal patterns and climate anomalies. These data are fundamental for urban planning, especially for dimensioning drainage systems and flood prevention.

Wind Patterns

Wind rose showing predominant direction and intensity

The analysis of wind patterns revealed predominant directions and seasonal variations, essential information for urban planning, pollutant dispersion, and urban microclimate studies. Our analysis identified natural wind corridors that can be preserved for better urban ventilation.

Meteorological Parameter Distribution

Temperature distribution (°C)

Humidity distribution (%)

We studied the statistical distribution of various meteorological parameters, including temperature, humidity, atmospheric pressure, and wind speed. This analysis allows us to identify local climate patterns and their variability, contributing to more accurate predictive models.
