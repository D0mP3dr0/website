Modeling and Evaluation of Radio Base Coverage through Graph Neural Networks

MBA Project in Data Science and Artificial Intelligence

See Exploratory Analysis See Models and Results

Project Overview

This research project develops an innovative approach to mobile network optimization, using Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to model and evaluate Radio Base Stations (RBS) coverage. The methodology combines advanced graph analysis techniques with deep learning to predict coverage quality and identify areas for network infrastructure optimization.

Graph Analysis

Modeling RBS networks as graphs, identifying communities, and calculating centrality metrics

Coverage Modeling

Accurate estimation of coverage areas based on real data and propagation models

Advanced Visualizations

Interactive maps and analytical graphs for interpreting the results obtained

GNN Models

Implementation of GraphSAGE, GCN, and GAT for coverage quality prediction

Key Results

Coverage Prediction

GAT model achieved 89.7% accuracy in predicting coverage quality

Network Optimization

Identification of 32 priority locations for new RBS installations

Community Analysis

Detection of 18 RBS communities with distinct characteristics

Model Comparison

GAT outperformed GCN and GraphSAGE by 12.3% in performance metrics
